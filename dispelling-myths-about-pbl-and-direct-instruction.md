# Dispelling Myths About PBL and Direct Instruction

> Linda Darling-Hammond discusses how well-placed direct instruction can scaffold rigorous, student-centered project-based learning.

**Source:** [https://www.edutopia.org/video/dispelling-myths-about-pbl-and-direct-instruction](https://www.edutopia.org/video/dispelling-myths-about-pbl-and-direct-instruction)

## Transcript

Linda: You know, often, project-based learning is posed as sort of antithetic to learning facts and direct instruction in skills. And yet, if you look in great classrooms, you see inquiry-based instruction and some strategic, appropriate, direct instruction for kids around things they need to know and learn, integrated with one another. And it’s really important that we understand that this is not part of one of the great curriculum wars, where we’re pitting, you know, whole language versus phonics, or math for understanding versus memorizing the math tables. It’s about creating useful inquiries and then strategically helping students get direct instruction when they need it, around particular skills or facts that they need to know, in the inquiry that they’re undertaking.
