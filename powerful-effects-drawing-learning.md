# The Powerful Effects of Drawing on Learning

> The science is clear: Drawing beats out reading and writing to help students remember concepts.

**Source:** [https://www.edutopia.org/video/powerful-effects-drawing-learning](https://www.edutopia.org/video/powerful-effects-drawing-learning)

## Article

Research has shown that drawing can be a potent tool for learning. It can help students understand content better and and retain information more effectively. But it’s not because of an innate learning style—that’s an outdated idea in education that has long been disproven.

Drawing taps into visual, kinesthetic, and linguistic areas of the brain at the same time, so information is processed in three different ways, establishing more connections across the brain’s neural network—and encoding learning more deeply.
